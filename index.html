A small rebellion against the noise

A little less hate.
A little more human.

Most people are actually pretty decent. We just don't see it that way anymore — because outrage gets clicks and the algorithm loves a fight. This page is our small rebellion. No politics. No drama. Just daily reminders that empathy still exists.

01  /  We need to talk

We need to talk about how much we hate each other.

Scroll through any comment section. Watch the news for 10 minutes. Read the replies under any opinion someone shares online. We've become really, really good at hating people we've never met. Different politics. Different beliefs. Different lifestyles. That's all it takes now.


This page exists to push back against that — not with more arguments, not with "our side vs. their side." Just with one simple reminder: the person on the other side of the screen is still a person. You don't have to agree with them. You just have to remember they're human.

02  /  Common ground

We're not as divided as we think.

Most people want the same things. Safety. Respect. A fair shot. We just forgot how to talk to each other. The headlines show the loudest voices. They're rarely the most common ones.


Start small. Listen first.

03  /  A choice

Hate travels faster than kindness.
That ends with us.

The algorithm rewards outrage. It always has. Anger keeps people scrolling, and scrolling keeps the lights on. Every share, every reaction, every comment is a small vote for the kind of internet — and the kind of world — we all have to live in. That's not a small thing. Most of the noise online isn't coming from most people. It's coming from the loudest ones. The rest of us have been quiet for too long. The next thing you share is a choice. Make it count.

You don't have to agree with everyone. You just have to remember they're human.
04  /  Disagreement

You can disagree without destroying.

Disagreement is healthy. It's how ideas get better, how societies move forward, how we figure out what actually matters. But there's a line — and we cross it constantly without noticing.


Disagreeing with someone's ideas is fair. Deciding they're a bad person because of those ideas is something else entirely. The moment we stop seeing the human behind the opinion, we've already lost the argument — and something more important than that.


You don't have to like everyone. You don't have to agree. You just have to remember they're human. That's it. That's the whole message.

05  /  Practice

Tolerance isn't weakness. It's the hardest thing you'll ever practice.

It's easy to be kind to people who are like you. It's easy to be patient with people who agree with you. Real tolerance — the kind that actually means something — is choosing to treat someone with dignity even when you think they're wrong. Even when they've been unkind first. Even when everything online is telling you they're the enemy.


Every day we choose what to amplify. A comment, a share, a reaction. Those choices add up. You don't have to agree with someone to treat them like a person. Start there. See what happens.

Who we are

Two friends who got tired of the noise.

We've been friends for over 15 years. And somewhere along the way, we both noticed the same thing: the world online started feeling meaner. Louder. More exhausting.

We're not activists. We're not politicians. We're just two people who believe the world is a little better when we treat each other like humans. So we made this page. Not to change the world — just to be a small corner of it that feels a little more human.

We're glad you're here.

— Sarah & Julia
